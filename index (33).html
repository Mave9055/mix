The Program

Built for the Room
Most Programs Walk Away From

A SAMHSA-aligned psychoeducational framework that shifts the clinical question from "what is wrong with you?" to "what happened to you?" — and makes that shift legible to peer specialists, not just clinicians.

Format

18 sessions — modular, sequential, 60–75 min each

Delivery

Peer-facilitated — no clinical license required

Primary Populations

Curriculum Architecture

Five-Part Roadmap

Eighteen sessions structured as a progression — not a topic list. Each part builds on the last.

Part I

How It Gets Built Into You

Early development, trauma formation, and the nervous system in childhood.

Part II

How It Destroys Your Relationships

Adult attachment disruption, trauma bonding, and relational re-enactment.

Part III

How You Tried to Cope

Regulation strategies, addiction, the breaking point, and high-functioning presentation.

Part IV

The Internal Battlefield

Neuroscience, shame, crisis architecture, and safety protocol.

Part V

Changing the World Around You

Integration, advocacy, meaning-making, and systemic change.
