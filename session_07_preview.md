# Session 07.5: Shut Up, I Have Work Tomorrow
## Public Preview Excerpt | Capitol Contracts LLC

### Overview
This session addresses the intersection of productivity, dissociation, and trauma. It is designed for workforce development programs, treatment courts, and veteran transition services.

### Key Concepts
* **Productivity as Dissociation**: How high-functioning presentation can mask deep-seated nervous system dysregulation.
* **The "Work Tomorrow" Defense**: Using professional obligations as a shield against emotional processing.
* **Functional Collapse**: The point where the "survival manual" of over-working fails.

### Facilitator Script Excerpt
> **Facilitator**: "We often hear that 'work is the best medicine.' And for many, it is a vital part of stabilization. But today, we're looking at the shadow side. We're looking at the moments when we use our jobs, our tasks, and our professional identities to stay disconnected from what our bodies are actually telling us."

### Participant Exercise: The Productivity Audit
1. Identify one professional task you use to avoid a personal emotion.
2. Describe the physical sensation in your body when you are "over-working."
3. What happens to that sensation when the workday ends?
